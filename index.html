UI/UX Design

Coffee Haven

A modern coffee-ordering platform letting users browse the menu, customise their drink, and place orders through a warm, tactile interface.

View project details
Problem

Ordering flows for small cafés are often generic and don't reflect the warmth of the brand. Customisation (milk, size, extras) was buried three screens deep in comparable apps.

Decision

Customisation options surface directly on the product card via an expandable sheet, so the most common changes (size, milk) take one tap instead of a full screen transition.

Outcome

A cohesive, single-brand ordering experience with a full flow from browse → customise → pay, built entirely in Figma with a reusable component library for menu cards.

Landing Page Design

GrowthSpace

A modern landing page for a digital marketing agency, built to showcase services and convert visitors into leads.

View project details
Problem

The agency's previous page buried its actual value proposition below three sections of stock imagery, and had no clear single call to action.

Decision

Led with a single, oversized value statement and one persistent "Get a proposal" CTA repeated at each scroll milestone, instead of competing CTAs per section.

Outcome

A clear top-to-bottom narrative: value proposition → services → social proof → single conversion path.

Home Screen Design

SweetBite — Donut Delight

A vibrant, playful home-screen design for a dessert delivery app, exploring bold colour and texture within a clean grid.

View project details
Problem

Dessert-delivery apps often default to muted, "safe" food-app palettes that undersell how fun and indulgent the product actually is.

Decision

Used a bento-style grid of saturated, category-coded colours so the home screen itself reads like a menu board, cutting down on text labels needed for browsing.

Outcome

A distinctive, colour-led home screen used as a design exploration piece — proof of range beyond utility-first UI.

UI/UX Design

Gohary Burger

A food-ordering concept for a local burger restaurant, focused on fast browsing and a frictionless checkout.

View project details
Problem

Local restaurant ordering apps often force users through a long category list before reaching a single popular item.

Decision

Surfaced a "Popular right now" row directly under the hero search bar, so the most-ordered items are one tap away instead of buried in categories.

Outcome

A streamlined menu-to-checkout flow with a visible order summary bar that persists as users keep browsing.
